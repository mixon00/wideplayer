About WidePlayer

WidePlayer makes small in-feed videos easier to watch. It widens supported videos on X and Mastodon while keeping the post, replies, and feed context on screen.

Why it exists

Social feeds are built around narrow columns. That works for text, but video often ends up cramped. Fullscreen is the other extreme: the video is large, but the post and surrounding context disappear.

WidePlayer fills the gap between those two states. It keeps the real player in use, gives it more room, and leaves the rest of the page understandable. No cloned video element. No separate player UI. No fullscreen jump.

The first version was built for X. Later updates added manual expand and collapse, smoother positioning, live width preview, per-platform settings, and Mastodon support for native videos and supported YouTube cards. Planned platforms stay marked as planned until they work well enough to ship.

Help

Where does WidePlayer work?

WidePlayer currently works on supported in-feed videos on X and Mastodon. Bluesky is in progress and LinkedIn is planned, but both stay disabled until support is ready.

Why doesn’t it appear on every video?

WidePlayer only touches players it can handle safely. It may skip media galleries, unusual embeds, heavily nested layouts, or videos where moving the original player would break controls or layout.

What does the popup toggle do?

The popup toggle enables or disables WidePlayer for that whole platform. If X is off, the extension will not add expand buttons or auto mode on X. The same applies to Mastodon.

What is Auto mode?

Auto mode widens supported videos while you scroll. It is platform-specific, so you can keep it on for one platform and off for another.

What is Manual mode?

Manual mode leaves videos in the normal feed layout until you press the expand button on a supported player. It is useful when you want WidePlayer available, but not automatic.

What does Preferred width change?

Preferred width controls how much wider the video can get. The final size is still limited by the viewport, video aspect ratio, and available screen space.

Why do I need to reload a supported platform?

Browser extensions inject content scripts when a page loads. If you install, update, or change some platform-level settings, reloading the page makes sure the current tab uses the latest code and settings.

Does WidePlayer replace the video player?

No. WidePlayer keeps the original player and moves it into a wider overlay. Playback state, native controls, volume, captions, and browser behavior stay attached to the same player.

Does WidePlayer collect data?

No. The extension stores settings in your browser and does not collect browsing history, account data, video data, or analytics from the extension.

Is WidePlayer open source?

Yes. WidePlayer is MIT licensed and the source code is available on GitHub.
